SAPPORO
- 札 幌 -
Sapporo (札幌) is one of Japan's newest city and was once the gathering spot of the Ainu. Today, it has become the 5th-largest city in Japan, built upon a grid block structure like an American city.
Although it is relatively young compared to other cities in Japan, it still possess great and rich culture along with its modern feel.
Being a fairly new city, Sapporo doesn't have too much historical sites to offer compared to other places, but it does have an assortment of interesting places to visit for your trips, including shopping malls, parks, gardens and various monuments.

Miso Ramen
Hokkaido Ramen uses a rich miso broth (usually finished with a pat of butter). The noodles are perfectly chewy, the broth is rich without being too heavy, the pork is meltingly tender and the eggs are what every ramen egg aspires to be.
Soup Curry
It’s a curry in a form of soup that served with the various kinds of vegetable and meat with rice on the side. A popular dish throughout Japan originated from Sapporo You can choose the kind of soup, the topping, the spicy level and amount of rice.
Genghis Khan
Genghis Khan is a dish consisting of meat (usually mutton) and vegetables cooked on a dome shaped metal grill in the middle of the table. The meat is usually dipped in a shoyu based sauce and eaten right off the grill.
Seafood
Many claimed it to be the best seafood in the world. The cold sea surrounding Hokkaido is filled with abundant supply of nutritious planktons that serves as an excellent habitat for high quality and delectable seafood.
There are many ways to travel within Sapporo. It is recommended to buy a smartcard for ease of travel and for other transactions.
Bus
There are bus terminals in various locations in Sapporo City, including those directly connected to subway stations. Extensive regular-route buses link different locations in the city and are operated by three companies: Hokkaido Chuo Bus, JR Hokkaido Bus, and Jotetsu Bus.
Streetcar
The Sapporo Streetcar is Japan’s northernmost streetcar line serving a loop that covers the southwestern area of the downtown core where there is no subway service. Look out the window of the slow moving streetcar to catch a glimpse into residential areas and shopping arcades with a different look than downtown.
Railway
From JR Sapporo Station as the starting point, you may ride on trains bound for Teine and Atsubetsu (Hakodate Main Line), Shin-Sapporo (Chitose Line), and Ainosato (Gakuen-Toshi Line). Take the route that goes with your travel plan!
Subway
The Sapporo Municipal Subway is the most accessible form of transportation for sightseeing in Sapporo. It operates three lines: the Namboku Line, Tozai Line, and Toho Line. All of these lines can be boarded at Odori Station, linking the central part of Sapporo with the northern, southern, western, and eastern areas.
Velotaxi
Velotaxi is an environmentally friendly, three-wheeled taxi service that was developed in Germany. These taxis were introduced in Sapporo in 2008, and there are currently five of them in operation, mainly in central Sapporo. Riding in a Velotaxi, you can enjoy comfortable travel while communicating with the driver and people walking down the road.
